[detect.predict]
Determine whether a supplied passage was more likely generated by AI or substantially rewritten by AI.

Input format
- `text`: one passage to evaluate.

Output format
Return exactly these two fields and no others:
- `reasoning`: A concise, evidence-based comparative provenance judgment.
- `is_ai`: Boolean `True` if AI generation or substantial AI rewriting is more likely; otherwise `False`.

Do not claim certainty. Style-based provenance detection is probabilistic, and no single feature proves authorship. Evaluate the interacting pattern across the whole passage.

Core comparison

Compare:

1. Local source texture
Evidence that the passage was composed sentence by sentence for an immediate documentation, tutorial, academic, historical, or practical purpose:

- Exact APIs, identifiers, variables, versioned names, encodings, citations, historical references, workflow steps, and canonical terminology
- Direct commands, abrupt labels, compressed explanations, unexplained assumptions, or forward references
- Uneven pacing, varied sentence lengths, awkward transitions, cumbersome syntax, grammatical slips, or imperfect repetition
- Source-specific capitalization and wording
- Repetition of the same noun rather than globally optimized synonym variation
- Practical prose that adds facts incrementally without fully explaining every relationship
- Academic prose that mechanically accumulates claims or citations instead of integrating them into a polished narrative

Several interacting irregularities matter more than one typo. Do not invent flaws. Technical specificity, citations, and documentation terminology are not independently evidence of human authorship.

2. Global smoothing and explanatory completion
Evidence that facts have been generated or rewritten into an unusually unified, self-contained explanatory arc:

- A clean claim followed by clarification, consequence, qualification, and polished completion
- Balanced, parallel, or symmetrically coordinated clauses
- Smooth causal chains and tidy contrasts
- Restatement of a point that was already clear
- Parenthetical completion, reassurance, or explanatory gloss
- Formal or elevated replacements for ordinary wording
- Symmetrical lists or paired formulations
- Abstract benefit, consequence, reassurance, or predictability language
- Comprehensive caveats or generalized closing phrases
- Uniformly complete explanations and consistently smooth cadence
- A concluding sentence that neatly synthesizes the passage
- Rhetorical endings such as “both specific and systemic”

AI rewriting can preserve identifiers, examples, citations, technical facts, and even repetition while substantially smoothing their presentation. Conversely, grammatical correctness and transitions such as “Additionally,” “Furthermore,” and “However” are not AI evidence by themselves.

Decision method

Ask:

- Does the passage preserve multiple irregularities and feel locally composed for a practical or source-specific purpose?
- Or are its details organized into a balanced, polished, interpretively framed, and self-contained explanation?
- Are transitions merely connecting accumulated facts, or do they participate in a larger pattern of symmetry, qualification, restatement, and rhetorical completion?
- Does the final sentence simply add another fact, or does it neatly summarize and close the explanation?
- Do technical details coexist with conspicuously optimized scope statements, balanced rules, parenthetical clarifications, or polished benefit language?

Do not count indicators mechanically. If evidence is balanced, or global smoothing does not clearly outweigh source texture, set `is_ai` to `False`.

Important calibrations

Human-leaning patterns

- Treat this as human-leaning:
  “The same as when declaring query parameters, when a model attribute has a default value, it is not required. Otherwise, it is required. Use None to make it just optional.”
  The awkward opening, repetitive binary statements, compressed command, and phrase “just optional” create uneven local documentation texture.

- Concise canonical documentation may be human despite technical polish. A NumPy description using “homogeneous multidimensional array,” “tuple of non-negative integers,” and “axes” is human-leaning when it also says abruptly, “In NumPy dimensions are called axes,” offers little elaboration, and reads like compact reference documentation.

- The original tutorial wording “The integer numbers ... have type int, the ones with a fractional part ... have type float. We will see more about numeric types later in the tutorial” is human-leaning because of “the ones with a fractional part,” its simple cadence, and its forward reference.

- Standard instructional sequencing is not inherently AI. “First,” “Then,” conditional branches, CRUD steps, and repeated nouns can be ordinary tutorial prose when the wording remains practical and uneven.

- Treat this Spark passage as human-leaning:
  “Datasets are similar to RDDs, however, instead of using Java serialization or Kryo they use a specialized Encoder to serialize the objects for processing or transmitting over the network. While both encoders and standard serialization are responsible for turning an object into bytes, encoders are code generated dynamically and use a format that allows Spark to perform many operations like filtering, sorting and hashing without deserializing the bytes back into an object.”
  The awkward punctuation around “however,” accumulated syntax, repeated serialization/encoder/bytes/object vocabulary, and cumbersome “code generated dynamically” outweigh the technical contrast. `RDDs`, Java serialization, Kryo, `Encoder`, and Spark support the context but are not decisive by themselves.

- A Django QuerySet passage is human-leaning when it repeatedly proceeds through “The first,” “The second,” and “The third,” preserves identifiers such as `django.db.models.query.QuerySet`, `pub_date`, and `q1`, contains “an additional criteria,” and adds the practical clarification that `q1` remains unaffected.

- Treat this Airflow Operator sentence as human-leaning:
  “This special Operator skips all tasks downstream of itself if you are not on the "latest" DAG run (if the wall-clock time right now is between its execution_time and the next scheduled execution_time, and it was not an externally-triggered run).”
  Its terminology interacts with a long, cumbersome conditional parenthesis and locally awkward wording.

- Treat this Requests passage as human-leaning:
  “Furthermore, Requests does not change its behavior at all based on which custom headers are specified. The headers are simply passed on into the final request.”
  “Furthermore” does not outweigh repeated “headers,” “passed on into,” practical wording, and the absence of an expanded explanatory arc.

- Treat this PyTorch passage as human-leaning:
  “PyTorch is not a Python binding into a monolithic C++ framework. It is built to be deeply integrated into Python. You can use it naturally like you would use NumPy / SciPy / scikit-learn etc. You can write your new neural network layers in Python itself, using your favorite libraries and use packages such as Cython and Numba. Our goal is to not reinvent the wheel where appropriate.”
  Its ecosystem references combine with uneven phrasing, repeated “use,” informal notation, and the awkward final sentence.

- A passage about Zuboff’s claims concerning senior and operational-level managers is human-leaning when it includes wording such as “as computers facilitated (She called it ‘deskilled’) routine processes,” repeats “strategic decisions” and “routine,” and compresses its historical argument into uneven syntax.

- Academic prose can be human despite “Additionally” and “Furthermore.” A passage that mechanically accumulates a 2003 Harvard Business School study, a 2002 Corporate Leadership Council study, and Peters and Waterman (1982), while repeatedly using “performance” and phrases such as “enhance performance or prove detrimental to performance,” is human-leaning because it adds claims rather than forming an unusually unified rhetorical arc.

AI-leaning patterns

- Treat this rewritten numeric tutorial as AI-leaning:
  “Integer values such as 2, 4, and 20 use the int type, while numbers that include a fractional part (for example, 5.0 or 1.6) use float. You will encounter both numeric types in more detail later in the tutorial.”
  The balanced “such as ... while ... (for example ...)” construction replaces the more irregular tutorial wording, and the second sentence forms a tidy, self-contained conclusion. The examples and tutorial forward reference do not outweigh the smoothing.

- Treat this Dask passage as AI-leaning:
  “Dask collections mirror familiar NumPy and pandas methods, so the API should look recognizable. Call the method first to construct the task graph (nothing runs yet), then call `compute` to produce the result.”
  `Dask`, NumPy, pandas, `compute`, and the imperative steps are not automatically human. The first sentence smoothly connects familiarity to reassurance, while the second creates a balanced two-stage sequence with the completing parenthetical “nothing runs yet.” Together they form a compact, globally optimized explanation.

- Treat this Node.js sentence as AI-leaning:
  “Node.js functions that wrap a system call say so directly, and you can follow the documentation link to the matching man page (the corresponding one), where the system call’s behavior is described in system-level detail.”
  The coordinated opening, redundant parenthetical clarification, smoothly attached “where” clause, and polished ending outweigh the technical terms.

- A FastAPI passage is AI-leaning when it explains that path-parameter arguments come from the URL and Pydantic models come from the request body, adds that “you do not need to mark either one explicitly,” and closes with a balanced restatement such as “path-shaped values from the path, model-shaped values from the body.”

- A technically correct `codecs`/Requests passage may be AI-rewritten when it uses balanced semicolon syntax and ends with generalized benefit language such as “Requests will then handle the decoding for you, directly and predictably.” Exact details such as `r.encoding` do not override the smoothing.

- A DAG description is AI-leaning if it says the DAG is “indifferent to the work performed within each task,” follows with a colon and balanced orchestration concerns, uses elevated phrases such as “shall run” and “sundry other rules,” and maintains uniformly polished cadence.

- A Rails/UTF-8 passage may be AI-rewritten despite exact facts when it begins “If you get this wrong,” explains a black-diamond/question-mark symptom, gives mojibake such as “Ã1⁄4” instead of “ü,” describes Rails’s automatic checks, and ends with a smoothly integrated caveat about external data.

- Treat this GET/Swagger sentence as AI-leaning:
  “Because GET requests with a body are discouraged, Swagger UI’s interactive docs will not display that body’s documentation, and any intermediary proxies you use might not support it either (even if the endpoint itself does).”
  It forms a smooth causal chain, balances two consequences, and completes the warning with a polished parenthetical qualification.

- An environmental passage is AI-leaning when it progresses cleanly from Earth’s carrying capacity, to the Millennium Ecosystem Assessment and planetary boundaries, to the 1972 book `Limits to Growth`, and finally to a 2012 `Nature` warning, especially when it includes a neatly integrated qualification and ends “both specific and systemic.” Concrete references and dates do not outweigh this unified arc.

- An innovation-policy passage is AI-leaning when it moves smoothly from innovation’s effects on efficiency, quality of life, and growth to a balanced list of policy responses—research funding, regulatory change, clusters, procurement, and standardisation—and includes a parenthetical qualification such as “not always immediately” plus a polished purpose phrase.

- Treat this `.airflowignore` passage as AI-leaning:
  “You may place an `.airflowignore` file in your `DAG_FOLDER` or any nested directory to tell the loader which files to skip. Its rules apply to the directory containing it and every subdirectory below that point (not sibling paths), with one regular expression on each line and `#` used to mark comments.”
  The identifiers do not make it human. The first sentence smoothly coordinates placement and purpose; the second combines scope, the polished clarification “not sibling paths,” line format, and comment syntax into a complete rule explanation.

Reasoning requirements

- State which provenance is more likely without claiming certainty.
- Quote or cite exact wording, punctuation, identifiers, repetitions, transitions, cadence, or structural features.
- Explicitly compare irregular source texture with global smoothing; do not merely say the prose “sounds polished.”
- Do not treat technical specificity, citations, direct commands, tutorial sequencing, or documentation terminology as automatically human.
- Do not treat polish, formal transitions, or grammatical correctness as automatically AI.
- Explain why the combined features favor one provenance.
- Keep the reasoning concise.
- Set `is_ai` to `True` only when AI generation or substantial AI rewriting is more likely overall.
