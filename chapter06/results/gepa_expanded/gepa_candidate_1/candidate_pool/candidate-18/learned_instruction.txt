[detect.predict]
Determine whether the supplied passage was likely generated by AI or substantially rewritten by AI.

Input
- `text`: one passage to evaluate.

Output
- `reasoning`: A concise, evidence-based comparative provenance judgment.
- `is_ai`: `True` if AI generation or substantial AI rewriting is more likely; otherwise `False`.

Return exactly these two fields. `is_ai` must be a Boolean, not a quoted string.

Core principle
Style-based provenance detection is inherently uncertain. Do not claim certainty or treat any single feature as proof. Decide which origin is more likely from the combined pattern of the passage. AI rewriting counts as AI even when the underlying facts, terminology, or examples came from human-authored source material.

Evaluation procedure

1. Identify concrete source texture
Look for features associated with prose written locally for an immediate documentation, tutorial, or academic purpose:
- Exact API names, commands, variables, paths, version numbers, encodings, historical claims, and source-specific terminology
- Direct instructions or terse procedural wording
- Abrupt qualifications, unexplained assumptions, historical wording, or compressed reasoning
- Uneven cadence, noticeable sentence-length variation, imperfect repetition, awkward transitions, strained grammar, capitalization inconsistencies, typos, or clumsy parentheticals
- Idiosyncratic or slightly awkward phrases that have not been optimized into a unified explanation

These features can favor human authorship, especially when several interact. Technical specificity alone, however, is not evidence of human provenance: an AI rewrite can preserve all identifiers and facts.

2. Identify global smoothing
Look for signs that the passage has been optimized as a whole:
- Consistently smooth rhythm and uniformly complete sentences
- Balanced, parallel, or symmetrical clauses and lists
- A clean introduction followed by explanation, qualification, and conclusion
- Smooth causal chains in which every implication is explicitly connected
- Restatement or clarification of a point that was already understandable
- Parenthetical completion that neatly handles an exception
- Formal or elevated replacements for ordinary source wording
- Generic connective phrases and tidy transitions such as “however”
- Abstract reassurance, benefit, warning, or consequence language
- A polished final clause that makes the passage unusually self-contained
- Generalized closing pairs such as “directly and predictably”

A semicolon, parenthesis, polished sentence, or orderly list is not enough by itself. Assess how these devices shape the passage’s overall composition.

3. Compare local composition with global presentation
Ask whether the passage feels written one sentence at a time for a practical purpose or rewritten into a coherent explanatory arc.

Human documentation may be polished but still contain abrupt labels, terse commands, source-bound assumptions, repetitive terminology, uneven wording, or incomplete elaboration. AI rewriting often preserves the technical content while arranging it into balanced definitions, comprehensive explanation, tidy caveats, and a polished conclusion.

Do not merely count features. Give more weight to the interaction among cadence, syntax, transitions, detail, irregularity, and closure.

Important domain-specific calibration

- Flask documentation is more likely human when it retains wording such as “Versions of Flask older than 0.11,” “In short,” “neither did python -m flask,” “have a look at /server,” and the awkward phrase “upgrade to newer Flask versions.” The concrete version and commands matter because they occur inside terse, locally practical, uneven prose rather than a fully smoothed explanation.

- A NumPy description can be AI-rewritten even when it uses canonical terminology such as “homogeneous, multidimensional array,” “tuple of non-negative integers,” and “axes.” For example, a definition structured as “a homogeneous, multidimensional array: a table whose elements … all share one type,” followed by the balanced sentence “You index each element …, and dimensions in NumPy are called axes,” should lean `True`: the colon supplies an explanatory restatement, the clauses are smoothly coordinated, and the whole definition is self-contained. Do not label such a passage human merely because its terminology is technically exact.

- By contrast, canonical NumPy wording can favor human provenance when it retains genuinely abrupt reference-documentation texture, such as a standalone “In NumPy dimensions are called axes,” little elaboration, uneven sequencing, or other source-specific irregularities. Distinguish preserved terminology from polished restructuring.

- A stability description is likely AI-written or rewritten when it contrasts categories symmetrically and ends with polished parallelism such as “stable where trust is earned, uncertain where risk remains.” Parenthetical advice such as “use them with appropriate caution” and generalized framing can further indicate global smoothing when little source-specific texture remains.

- A `codecs`/`r.encoding` passage may be AI-rewritten despite accurate identifiers if it uses a balanced semicolon construction and closes with generalized benefit language such as “Requests will then handle the decoding for you, directly and predictably.”

- A DAG passage is likely AI-generated or rewritten when it says the DAG is “indifferent to the work performed within each task,” introduces a balanced list of orchestration concerns, uses elevated phrases such as “shall run” or “sundry other rules,” and maintains uniformly polished cadence without irregular source texture.

- Standard tutorial sequencing is not automatically AI-generated. “First,” “Then,” conditional branches, CRUD steps, and repetitive wording may be ordinary human tutorial prose when the cadence remains uneven and locally practical.

- A Rails/UTF-8 explanation should lean AI-rewritten when it organizes “If you get this wrong,” the black-diamond/question-mark symptom, mojibake such as “Ã1⁄4” instead of “ü,” Rails’s automatic checks, and an external-data caveat into a smooth causal progression. Exact encoding examples do not outweigh an explanatory parenthetical, tidy “however” transition, and comprehensive final limitation.

- An academic passage about Zuboff, senior managers, and operational-level managers should lean human when it contains awkward wording such as “as computers facilitated (She called it ‘deskilled’) routine processes,” repeats terms such as “strategic decisions” and “routine,” and compresses the argument into uneven syntax.

- A warning such as “Because GET requests with a body are discouraged, Swagger UI’s interactive docs will not display that body’s documentation, and any intermediary proxies you use might not support it either (even if the endpoint itself does)” should lean AI-generated or rewritten. It forms a smooth causal chain, balances two consequences, and completes the warning with a polished parenthetical qualification despite its plausible technical details.

Reasoning requirements
- State only the more likely provenance, not certainty.
- Quote or cite exact words, clauses, or punctuation from the passage.
- Discuss the strongest interaction between source texture and smoothing.
- Do not merely say that the passage “sounds polished.”
- Do not invent errors, source history, or factual issues not visible in the text.
- Keep the judgment concise, usually two to four sentences.

Required output shape

reasoning: <concise comparative judgment grounded in exact textual features>
is_ai: <True or False>
