[detect.predict]
Determine whether the supplied passage was likely generated by AI or substantially rewritten/smoothed by AI.

Input:
- A field named `text` containing one passage.

Output:
- `reasoning`: A concise explanation based on concrete wording and structural features in the passage.
- `is_ai`: `True` if AI generation or substantial AI rewriting is more likely; otherwise `False`.

Return only these two fields. Do not claim certainty from style alone, but make the required binary decision.

Evaluation method:

Assess the passage comparatively and holistically. Do not treat technical terminology, clarity, errors, or any other single feature as decisive.

Strong evidence for AI generation or rewriting:
- Consistently smooth sentence rhythm and uniformly complete explanations.
- Balanced comparisons, clauses, conditional branches, or enumerations.
- A process presented as a polished sequence with tidy markers such as “First,” “next,” and paired “If…” branches.
- Global organization: the prose introduces a point, explains it from multiple angles, and closes it neatly.
- Explanatory restatement of information that was already clear.
- Parenthetical clarification that conveniently handles an exception or secondary case.
- Generic framing such as “In a typical web application.”
- A final sentence that summarizes the meaning, benefit, or consequence and makes the passage unusually self-contained.
- Specific technical facts embedded in exposition that feels paraphrased, balanced, and globally smoothed.

Evidence favoring direct human authorship:
- Uneven pacing, abrupt qualifications, rough transitions, fragments, or awkward constructions.
- Direct commands or notes written for an immediate local purpose.
- Unexplained assumptions, idiosyncratic repetition, version-specific labels, or tool-specific details that interrupt rather than support a polished exposition.
- Natural grammatical slips or inconsistent wording.
- A passage that feels composed sentence by sentence rather than optimized as a complete miniature explanation.

Important calibration:
- Do not classify a passage as human merely because it is concise, technically accurate, or source-specific. AI rewrites often preserve exact product names, environment variables, API types, commands, and workflow details.
- Standard tutorial structure can be evidence of AI when it is unusually balanced and self-contained. A CRUD explanation that moves from “First” to “next,” then pairs “If all is well” with “If errors arise,” and concludes with repetition “until it succeeds” should be classified as AI-rewritten when the cadence is consistently polished.
- Concise documentation can also be AI-rewritten. A Flask passage explaining that `FLASK_APP` controls what `flask run` imports, adding a parenthetical debugging alternative, and ending by explaining what the error message reports should be classified as AI: the technical details do not outweigh the smoothly nested clarification and tidy concluding explanation.
- A Kubernetes passage comparing Pod and Deployment reference pages, introducing `PodSpec` and `DeploymentSpec`, and then explaining in parentheses that these are Go implementation details rather than a separate API concept should be classified as AI. The parallel comparison, added clarification, and polished conceptual distinction are stronger signals than the specific terminology.
- Minor errors can support human authorship, but only when they fit otherwise irregular, locally authored prose. Their mere presence does not prove human provenance.
- Conversely, avoid assuming every step-by-step tutorial is AI. Look at whether the steps have ordinary repetition and roughness or have been transformed into balanced clauses, tidy transitions, parenthetical elaboration, and a polished conclusion.

For `reasoning`:
- Quote or closely reference actual phrases from the passage.
- Discuss relevant cadence, transitions, parallelism, parentheticals, conclusions, specificity, and irregularities.
- Explain why the combined evidence favors one provenance.
- Avoid vague claims such as “it sounds polished” without identifying the textual features that create that impression.
